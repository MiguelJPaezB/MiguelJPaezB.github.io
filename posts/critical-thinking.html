Critical Thinking and Media Literacy

In today’s world, we are surrounded by information — on social media, TV, news websites, and even memes. Sometimes it’s difficult to know what’s true and what’s not. That’s why critical thinking and media literacy are so important.

These skills help us analyze, evaluate, and understand the messages we see every day. Instead of believing everything immediately, we can question it, verify sources, and make our own conclusions.

How Media Can Influence Our Thinking

Media has a strong influence on our emotions, opinions, and even our decisions. Advertisements, news, and influencers often try to persuade us in subtle ways. Developing media literacy allows us to detect bias, recognize manipulation, and think independently.

For example, a video or post can make us feel angry or excited, even if the information is not complete or true. When we use critical thinking, we pause and ask: “Who created this message?”, “Why?”, and “Is it reliable?”.

My Reflection

I think being a critical thinker means not accepting information blindly. It’s about being curious, asking questions, and searching for the truth. Media literacy helps me identify when something is fake or exaggerated.

As a student of software analysis and development, I often use the internet to research or learn new things. Thanks to critical thinking, I can choose better sources and understand technology in a more responsible way.

What I Learned

Through this post, I practiced expressing opinions and connecting ideas in English using logical connectors like “because”, “so”, and “therefore”. I also learned new vocabulary related to communication, reasoning, and media.

This activity reminded me that learning a language is not only about grammar, but also about understanding the world and how we communicate within it.

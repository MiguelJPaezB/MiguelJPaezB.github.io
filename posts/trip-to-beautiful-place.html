A Trip to a Beautiful Place in My Region

Colombia is full of amazing natural places, and one of the most special for me is Las Tinas, a small rural area near Corozal, Sucre. My mother’s family is from there, and it’s a place full of memories, peace, and nature.

At the beginning of the year, I took a short trip to visit my mother, who lives there with my grandparents.
The landscape of the Sucre savanna is beautiful — vast fields where the land blesses the farmers with good harvests, clean air, and kind people everywhere.

During the day, it was very hot — perfect weather to sit under a mango tree eating watermelon.
At night, the early-year breeze made the temperature drop, ideal for going to the main square to hang out with friends and watch the older men playing parqués, dominoes, and cards.
That’s how I enjoyed the calm and simplicity of life far from the city.

My Experience During the Trip

During my visit, I spent time with my mother and other relatives.
The day I arrived, my mom welcomed me with mote de queso (my favorite dish).
We talked for hours and shared stories about our lives.

I also visited my friends who live in nearby villages, took many pictures and videos, and helped my grandmother take care of her garden.
With my grandfather, I went to the fields, where we harvested cassava, yam, mango, tamarind, and passion fruit.
We even hunted a rabbit and went fishing with my uncle Carlos.

It was a great opportunity to disconnect from technology and enjoy nature.
I realized how important it is to take time for myself and appreciate the little things:
the song of the birds, the wind, and the peace that comes from being close to family.

What I Learned

Writing about my trip helped me practice the past simple tense to describe experiences and actions from the past. It also helped me use adjectives to describe places and express feelings.

This trip reminded me of the importance of family, nature, and taking time to rest — things that are sometimes forgotten in daily life.
